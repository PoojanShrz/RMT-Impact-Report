Mohini Khatri

Favorite Destination in Nepal

There are many destinations to choose from, but it has to be Pashupatinath Temple as it holds religious, cultural, traditional and historical significance. No matter how many times I visit the temple, the impact of visiting this site is always different and emotional.

Favorite memory from a trip

I was with a guest who was in her 80s. We were at Pashupatinath temple where we were observing post death rituals and how Hindus bid farewell to a departed soul for the last time. My guest got emotional and expressed that these rituals reminded her of losing her husband whom she missed dearly. She told me that life is beautiful and that we must cherish what we have. Traveling made her feel alive and being at the temple that day helped her let go of her emotions. I remember that day because it made me feel a special human connection that transcends cultures and nationalities.

Ganga Adhikari

Favorite Destination in Nepal

Annapurna Base Camp and Mardi Himal Base camp

Favorite memory from a trip

My favorite memory is from Mardi when I saw the mountains from so close that it felt like a wall of mountain right in front of my eyes. Also, the people I went with were great which made the whole trip better.

Shristi Shrestha

Favorite Destination in Nepal

Annapurna Base Camp for the varied landscape underneath the snowy peaks.

Favorite memory from a trip

One of my favorite memories is accompanying and motivating two older ladies in our group throughout one trek. This experience turned out to be a good lesson that I carry with me. I also enjoyed playing different roles like a friend, motivator, singer, dancer, guardian and a leader.

Besides this, I also appreciated learning from a female tour leader, Ms. Kalpana Bhattarai whom I admire very much for her strength, dedication, confidence and grace.
