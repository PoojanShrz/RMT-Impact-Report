Junu Shrestha

Favorite Destination in Nepal

Among the places I have visited, Upper Mustang is my favorite. It is an isolated place with a unique culture. Reaching the destination in itself is amazing, like entering a different country. Dhakmar, Lomanthang and Jhong caves are my favorite places in Upper Mustang. While the lovely, warm hearted people welcoming you with a smile make you feel at home, the buddhist flags, the spinning wheels and the monasteries make you feel at peace.

Favorite memory from a trip

Every trip is memorable for me. I enjoy meeting new people with different cultures, traditions, stories and experiences from around the world. It feels like the world comes to meet me in Nepal.

Jamuna Khatri

Favorite Destination in Nepal

Nagarkot because it is the best place to see the eight popular ranges in the Himalayas. I feel very refreshed after visiting Nagarkot, which also reminds me of my childhood.

Favorite memory from a trip

When an older person in a group I was leading referred to me as “Aama” (which means mother in Nepali) for taking care of him and the whole group like a mother. As he kept calling me “Aama”, the entire group also started calling me that. I felt a special emotional connection with all of the travelers as they were sad to leave Nepal and me.

Monica K.C.

Favorite Destination in Nepal

Pokhara because this place can offer a complete package consisting of adventure, nature, culture, religion and entertainment, among others. I also like Barauli Community Homestay.

Favorite memory from a trip

I have had lots of good memories while leading a trip but the one that I recall instantly is during one of my Annapurna trips when I was leading a group of 8 people. Throughout the trip, there were several incidents that took place as we walked from Ghandruk to Landruk that weren’t necessarily good but the way they happened gave us something to laugh and talk about. It was a rainy day, making the trails slippery and infested with leeches. All of us were struggling to get downhill and had nasty leech bites. Somehow we kept moving with the help of our porters and our assistant guide. Although everyone was strictly instructed to not take pictures while walking and to watch our steps, a young woman was taking a video. She missed a step and tumbled down a cliff. Luckily, we had almost reached the base so the steepness was not dangerous. As our assistant guide went to help her, she jumped on him and both of them started rolling down the cliff further. Others started laughing but were also scared at the same time. Fortunately, there were no serious injuries and all of us made it to our destination.

As we settled down and talked about the day, our stomachs started cramping because of laughing too much. What had happened could have been serious but at the end of the day it brought about lots of laughter considering how the events unfolded, making the trip memorable.
